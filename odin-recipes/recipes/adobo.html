Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken – In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, and crushed garlic. Mix well, cover, and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor).
  2. Sear the chicken – Heat a pot or deep pan over medium heat. Add a little oil (optional), then take the chicken pieces out of the marinade and sear them until lightly browned on all sides. This step adds more flavor.
  3. Add the marinade – Pour the marinade (soy sauce and garlic) back into the pot with the chicken.
  4. Add vinegar (do not stir yet) – Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer uncovered for a few minutes without stirring. This helps cook off the strong acidic taste.
  5. Add the rest of the ingredients – Drop in the bay leaves, peppercorns, and water. Mix gently.
  6. Simmer – Cover the pot and let it simmer over low to medium heat for about 30–40 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender. Stir occasionally.
  7. Adjust seasoning – Taste the sauce and add salt and pepper as needed. If you prefer it a bit thicker, continue simmering uncovered until the sauce reduces to your liking.
  8. Serve – Transfer to a serving dish and enjoy with steamed rice.
